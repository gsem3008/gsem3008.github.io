About Troop 3008

What is Girl Scouting?

Girl Scouting is a program established by the Girl Scouts of the United States of America (GSUSA) for girls who are grades K-12. GSUSA has been in continuous operation since 1912 when it was founded in Savannah, Georgia, by Juliette Gordon Low. The National headquarters of the GSUSA are now located in New York City. Girl Scouting is nonprofit—adult, unpaid volunteer leaders work with the girls in the program, and are supported by paid staff and professionals in local area councils.

How Is Girl Scouting Organized?

Girls in the GSUSA are organized into grade-appropriate levels:

  • DaisyGrades K -1
  • BrownieGrades 2-3
  • JuniorGrades 4-5
  • CadetteGrades 6-8
  • SeniorGrades 9-10
  • AmbassadorGrades 11-12

Troop 3008 will only enroll girls in the Daisy through Junior levels; Currently we have 17 Daisy Grade 1 members enrolled.

GSUSA has headquarters in New York, but is divided nationally into local area Girl Scout Councils. Girl Scout Troop 3008 belongs to District 12 of the Girl Scouts of Eastern Missouri (GSEM), headquartered in St. Louis, MO.

GSEM has 53,781 members: 40,419 girls and 13,362 adults in eastern Missouri, which includes St. Louis City and the 28 surrounding Missouri counties of Adair, Audrain (Van-Far School District), Clark, Crawford, Franklin, Iron, Jefferson, Knox, Lewis, Lincoln, Linn, Macon, Marion, Monroe, Pike, Putnam, Ralls, Reynolds, Schuyler, Scotland, Shelby, St. Charles, St. Francois, St. Louis, Ste. Genevieve, Sullivan, Warren, and Washington.

What is Troop 3008?

Girl Scout Troop 3008 organized during the fall of 2018. At the moment is is a single-level group, growing with the girls as they progress through the grade levels. The troop program focuses on the many different badges and patches girls have available to them through Girl Scouting at their current program level, as well as allowing the girls to participate in outings, field trips, and other fun stuff.

Troop 3008 meets twice a month, once as a troop meeting for badge/petal work and once as an outing/activity to further educate the current badge.

Adult Leadership

Many willing girls each year are unable to join Girl Scouting because not enough adults volunteer to be leaders. Having leaders for the various activities of Troop 3008 is very important, as well as non-leader parent support. Girl Scouting is a volunteer-driven organization. Everyone, including parents, is expected to pitch in for the girls. In this way, Girl Scouting is different than many other after-school activities.

  • Direct Leadership GSUSA requires Troop 3008 to have a minimum leadership team of at least a primary leader and a co-leader, one of whom must be an adult female. In addition to these two mandatory positions, we also have two adult leaders to help with onsite activities.
  • Parent Helpers/Volunteers Parents, male or female, are needed at all non-meeting levels of the Troop 3008 program. Parents will need to step forward for campouts, driving, field trips, and cookie sales. Except for troop meetings and overnighters, parent helper/volunteers are not required to become registered members of Girl Scouts, and do not need to be background checked. However, our Council does encourage parent helper/volunteers to be registered to support the council and GSUSA membership.
  • Adults to Overnight Camp with Troop ANY adults who want to camp overnight with Troop 3008, including parents, must be a registered member of the Council and have an approved background check. If not otherwise registered as a direct leader, then these adults register as parent/volunteers and initiate a background check.

Parent helper/volunteers who act ONLY as drivers, (i.e. help with the transportation of girls on Troop trips/outings), are required to be become registered members of Girl Scouts, complete with the background check. Drivers must also have a valid driver’s license, proof of current insurance and inspection for vehicle that will be used, as well as be 21 years of age or older.

Check out the Volunteer section for detailed instructions on joining Troop 3008 as an adult parent/volunteer and/or direct leader

Finances and Expenses

While leaders and parents volunteer their time for Girl Scouting, Girl Scouting itself is not free. There are individual charges for membership, badges and activities, plus the troop receives program support from GSEM. Troop dues are $60 per year. Here are some of the expenses that girls and their families should be prepared to pay for:

  • Registration The annual $25 GSUSA membership fee is collected every fall (or in the late spring, if the member decides to do “early bird” renewal registration). The GSUSA membership fee is collected online through the myGS system. The $25 GSUSA membership fee goes directly to GSUSA, and does not pay for any of the Troop 3008 activities, badges and awards. The cost of registration is not included in the troop dues.
  • Uniform Daisy Tunic, with (1) Flag Patch, (2) Troop Numbers 3-0-0-8, (3) Insignia Tab, and (4) Membership Pin Set. Troop 3008 encourages the wearing of the tunic at all meetings and functions. The cost of the uniform is not included in the troop dues. See the Troop 3008 Uniform page for more information.
  • Outdoor Program and Activities Troop 3008 has "offsite" fieldtrips and activities once a month. These outdoor activities are usually done on a “pay-as-you-go” system, instead of being done through troop dues. Outdoor activity fees could include things like a program fee (for set activities at Girl Scout camping facilities, or fees for using a state park), plus food costs and infrastructure costs. Sometimes the troop is able to cover a portion or all of the costs, however this is not the default.
  • Cookie Money Each year, in the January-February time frame, Troop 3008 participates in the annual Cookie sale. The more boxes of cookies the girls sell, the more cookie money will be allocated for the exclusive use of the troop. Unlike the meeting costs (that the troop provides for) the use of cookie money to offset the costs of activities is a decision of the girls in the troop.
  • Council and Area Sponsored Activities Each year, the Council provides information listing activities that the Council has available for girls individually in their levels, as well as for troops. These activities usually have a cost, although some are free. Depending on whether the girl goes by herself, or with her level, or as a troop, will depend on how these are paid.
  • Summer Camps GSEM operates several staffed summer resident camps, offering programs for all levels, and programs which range all the way from a day trip to a couple of weeks of resident camp. These programs are spelled out in summer guides that are distributed each year to all registered Girl Scouts, as well as available online.
  • Day Camp Troop 3008 has not participated yet in a Day Camp, however we are open to the experience!
How to Pay for Girl Scouting in Troop 3008

The money to cover the expenses of Girl Scouting will come from the following sources:

  • DonationsThe Council does not allow Troop 3008 to solicit donations, but it does not prohibit the troop from accepting them. Donations in excess of $250 must be given as an earmark to the Girl Scouts of Eastern Missouri Council, which in turn will send the earmarked funds back to Troop 3008.
  • Annual DuesTroop 3008 dues are $60 per year - see below under dues.
  • Pay-Go Additional costs for outings and activities - see above under expenses
  • Fund RaisersGirl Scouts strongly encourage the girls themselves to start early, and learn how to earn and pay for their activities in Girl Scouting. This can be done through family allowances with set jobs, and paying dues as part of allowance. The Troop can also put together fundraisers in which the girls can participate - things like yard sales, for example. Fundraisers other than product sales must be approved by the Troop 3008 leaders committee and by the Council.
  • Official GS Product SalesAnother way to raise funds is through the officially sanctioned product sales. There are two of these per year - the famous cookie sales in the January-February time frame and the fall product sales. Troop 3008 encourages, but does not require, each girl to participate in one or more of the product sales. From all funds earned from product sales, Troop 3008 will retain 100% and allows the girls to decide what to do with the money they have earned as a troop. Cookie money, for example, could be used to pay any of the expenses listed above, for a service project, a charitable donation, or for a special outing or trip.

Safety Checkpoints/Volunteer Essentials/Volunteer Policies

Girl Scouting has always prescribed a set of national safety rules for girls and leaders to follow in the Girl Scout experience. These are set out in the Volunteer Essentials, the Volunteer Policies, and the Safety Activity Checkpoints. These resources should be used to determine GS policies and procedures for any particular situation. When in doubt, leaders contact the Council for a ruling on matters which may not be covered in these resources

Diversity and Inclusivity

You may have heard about controversies within Scouting over religion and sexual orientation. These controversies only affect the Scouting program for boys, known as the Boy Scouts of America (BSA). In years past, the BSA took several policy stands regarding exclusionary membership rules for its youth and adult membership, such as a required belief in God or a supreme being, and denial of youth or adult membership based on sexual orientation. Within the past few years, BSA has amended its policies to allow youth and adult membership in BSA regardless of sexual orientation, but has retained the requirement for a belief in God or a supreme being. BSA has also retained the option of its chartering partner organizations to exclude youth and adult members on sexual orientation basis, if the chartering organization has a religious objection.

The Girl Scouts of the USA, of which Troop 3008 is a part, is expressly diverse and inclusive, and does not subscribe to any discriminatory or exclusionary membership policies. Girl Scouting is open to all - well, except for youth males.

On youth membership, BSA made the controversial decision to allow girls to join its Cub Scouting programs (grades 1-5), starting the fall of 2018, and officially changed their name to Scouts BSA. Troop 3008 will remain strictly within Girl Scouting/GSUSA. Girl Scouting itself remains just for girls, no youth male members. Girl Scouting does allow transgender girls to join Girl Scouting, on a case-by-case basis.
